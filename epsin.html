Larry Ellison

Born : 17 August (1944-present)

The American businessman and entrepreneur Larry Ellison (Lawrence Joseph Ellison) was the co-founder and chief executive officer (1977–2014) of the software company – Oracle Corporation. He step-down from Oracle’s CEO role after 37 years at the helm in 2014. Oracle corporation grew in part through steady acquisitions of software companies, Oracle got the cloud revenue with $9.3 billion for NetSuite in 2016.

Early Life

He was born in New York City, to an unwed Jewish mother. His biological dad was an Italian-American United States Army Air force pilot. After Ellison contracted pneumonia at the age of 9 months, his mom gave him to her aunt’s home for adoption. Larry Ellison never graduated, he dropped out of two consecutive colleges.

Larry Ellison’s adoptive dad many times told him “you are good for nothing”, but Larry Ellison’s “Never Say Die Attitude” made him the world’s 3rd richest person in the US with an estimated net worth of $54 billion.

Hunger Of Success

Larry Ellison saw many challenges and rejection, but there is a voice inside everyone that helps to obtain something bigger than your expectations. Larry Ellison was frequently changing his jobs. In 1977, with Bob Miner and Ed Oates (Larry co-workers), he started his 1st business Software Development Laboratories (SDL).
